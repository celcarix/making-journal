## Review and Reflect 

Over the past few weeks of experimentation, what I enjoyed most was documenting my daily life and transforming these experiences into data. Through organisation and visualisation, I could re-examine my life and make previously fragmented experiences more structured and meaningful. I also particularly enjoyed the terminal-related experiments, especially generating ASCII art animations through procedural code. This made me realise that data is not an object to be analysed, but can also become a form of visually expressive creation. This process of combining technology and creation was very interesting to me. Currently, I don’t have a very clear direction for development, but I am particularly interested in p5.js. While I'm new to coding, its potential in interactive and visual presentation makes me want to explore it further. Through these practical experiences, I gradually understand that data is not just objective information, but a medium that can be designed and interpreted. This also echoes the concept of data humanism mentioned in class, allowing data to connect with personal experiences and emotions. Based on the above experiments, I hope to combine daily data with interactive visualisations and develop a more personalised way of presenting data.

## Thematic Focus and Data Source(s)

I hope to explore how emotions and personal experiences in daily life, especially “moments of joy,” can be recorded and transformed into data. I want to focus on a very common but often overlooked experience, representing it through data to make it visible and meaningful. Especially when people nowadays focus too much on the negative side of things and ignore the positive side of life, it would be inspiring to those who feel lost. In the future, this system could also support mental well-being by making moments of happiness visible, helping people reconnect with positive experiences in their daily lives. I envision a system where people can continuously record their emotions and daily experiences. This data will not only be used for analysis but can be transformed into visual and interactive representations to help people rediscover their lives. Regarding the data source, I plan to use data I collect myself, such as recording the moments of happiness and emotional changes. These data will be personal and continuously accumulated, and may incorporate simple categorization to make it easier to transform and visualize. In terms of presentation, I hope to combine programmatic generation and interactive design, such as p5.js or terminal visuals, to transform this data. This project also responds to classroom discussions on data humanisms and data drawing, focusing on how personal data can be experienced through visual and interactive forms.


## Visualisation & Impact
In this project, I want to use p5.js to create an interactive system that visualises recorded moments of happiness into a circular graphic, changing color according to the type. For example, blue for food and orange for friends. The purpose of this work is to visualise happiness, making it easier for people to notice and focus on the positive moments in life, rather than solely focusing on negative emotions. I hope that the viewers will become more conscious of doing things that make them happy, and also more likely to perceive the joy in life, rather than overlooking positive experiences in everyday life.
